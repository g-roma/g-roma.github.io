Hacks

I enjoy exploring new concepts and applications without the burden of long-term commitment. Most of the projects here have been put together in a matter of hours during music-related hackathons. There is always so much to learn from trying out crazy ideas quickly.

  • NMF Randomiz: process drum loops by a shuffled Non-negative Matrix Factorization of the spectrogram. Produces a rhythmic filtering effects driven by the drum pattern. Science of Music Hackathon (HAMR2016) (With Anna Xambó).

  • Gangsta Headbang: a fedora hat with an embedded arduino Flora board, accelerometers and LEDs that triggers music and light in sync with your head movements. Barcelona Music Hack Day 2015 (with Anna Xambó).

  • CrowDJ: A DJ session where the selection is controlled collectively. The app was built using Meteor framework and the Rdio API (now gone). Barcelona Music Hack Day 2014 (with Anna Xambó). Rdio prize.

  • Soundscape Turntablism: Control sound speed by interacting with a servo-motor holding a reactable fiducial. Barcelona Music Hack Day 2012 (with Anna Xambó). Reactable and Zvooq prizes.

  • Soundscape DJ: DJing soundscapes from Freesound using a dtouch-style tangible interface. London Music Tech Fest 2012 (with Anna Xambó). Warp Records prize.

  • Morning Singer: a virtual singer that could sing any news feed (parsed from RSS) using vocaloid as a web service (implemented in the short-lived Canoris API by Music Technology Group at UPF). Barcelona Music Hack Day 2011. Canoris and Bmat prizes.
