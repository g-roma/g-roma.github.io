Dub Life

Dub life is an algorithmic dub techno music generator written in SuperCollider. Dub techno is quite a narrow and predictable genre. It can be seen as a continuous repetition of the music released by Basic Channel in the mid-90s. Yet there is a lot of music being produced nowadays in this genre. The idea that it should be easy to model and partially automate. The program is based in a database of sounds and patterns, and a very simple grammar for dance-oriented electronic music sessions. Dub life is still work in progress, but it already achieved the second price in the AlgoRhythms competition sponsored by the Neukom Institute at Dartmouth.
